Here’s a quick analysis of the 13.3 Open Workout. The workout is an AMRAP in 12 minutes of

  • 150 Wall balls (WB)
  • 90 Double-unders (DU)
  • 30 Muscle-ups (MU)

I will look at RX scores only.

The WOD comes with two movements that require technical skill: double-unders and muscle-ups so we’d expect these to be natural points where people stop. For example if you cannot perform a muscle-up, but can do wall balls and double-unders sufficiently well, you will hit 240 reps (150 wall balls, 90 double unders).

This workout actually came with a tie-breaker: if two athletes hit 240 repetitions each, the one how did so earlier scores more points. But scoring that first muscle-up at rep 241 will put you above all others with 240 reps! How many points will this give you on the leaderboard? Well let’s have a look at the scored repetitions from this workout:

That’s a lot of athletes hitting the 240 mark, both for female and male athletes (though the effect is more pronounced for females, as muscle-ups typically also require a lot of upper-body strength).
Table 1: Athletes scoring 150 WB + 90 DU
gender total fraction [%]
female 7253 23.81
male 6093 12.78

This is how much having at least one muscle-up (and making it through the 150 WB and 90 DU) were worth in 13.3: 7253 places for females and 6093 for males (there were a lot more male athletes in this open which explains why the fractions are so far apart).
